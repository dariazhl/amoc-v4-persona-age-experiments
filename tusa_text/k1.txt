Liza, Ann, and Peter Klein wanted to go to the river falls. The river falls had the best rocks for climbing. "We'll go on saturday," Dad and Mom said. Ann and Peter were good at climbing. They knew how to climb with a rope. Liza did not know how to climb rocks. She wanted to learn to climb as well as her brother and sister. Dad showed Liza how to use a rope. Mom and Ann and Peter showed Liza pictures of some rocks they had climbed. They told her how much fun it was to climb. Liza was afraid of climbing. But she didn't say anything. Saturday came. Liza helped Dad take everything out to the car. She wondered about telling him that she was afraid. But Liza didn't say anything. When the Kleins were at the river falls, Peter said, "we can start at the rocks over there. They don't look hard to climb." Peter put the rope around a big rock. He used the rope to climb up the rocks. Ann and Liza ran to Peter and started climbing. "Come on, Mom and Dad. It's fun," Liza shouted. It wasn't as hard as she thought it would be. Up and down the rocks the Kleins climbed. They were having a good time. Dad stayed beside Liza. Mom, Ann, and Peter went first. The rocks were starting to get harder to climb. Liza was pleased with herself. She was doing well. "This one will be hard," Ann said as they came to a steep hill. Liza looked at it.
